Section 1.1: Thinking Mathematically

Understanding how mathematical thinking differs from everyday thinking

Key Concepts (Think Like a 5th Grader!)

Deductive Reasoning

What it is: Starting with big rules and figuring out specific answers

Example:

  • ‚Ä¢ All birds have feathers
  • ‚Ä¢ A robin is a bird
  • ‚Ä¢ Therefore: Robins have feathers

Inductive Reasoning

What it is: Looking at patterns and making predictions

Example:

  • ‚Ä¢ I've seen 10 swans
  • ‚Ä¢ All 10 were white
  • ‚Ä¢ Therefore: All swans are white

Study Tips & Tricks

1

Deductive: Think "top-down" - big rules to specific answers

2

Inductive: Think "bottom-up" - patterns to predictions

3

Practice: Look for these types of reasoning in news, ads, and everyday conversations

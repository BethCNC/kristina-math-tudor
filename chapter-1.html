1.1

Thinking Mathematically

Deductive vs. Inductive Reasoning

15-20 min

Deductive Reasoning

Drawing conclusions from general principles. If the premises are true, the conclusion MUST be true.

Example:
• All birds have feathers (general principle)
• A robin is a bird (specific case)
• Therefore: Robins have feathers (conclusion)

Inductive Reasoning

Making predictions based on patterns or observations. Conclusions are probable but not guaranteed.

Example:
• I've seen 10 swans and they're all white (observation)
• Therefore: All swans are white (prediction)
• Note: This is actually false—black swans exist!
Key Difference:
  • • Deductive: General → Specific (100% certain if premises are true)
  • • Inductive: Specific → General (probable, not certain)

Estimating and Evaluating

Strategies for quick approximations and evaluating reasonableness

15-20 min

Estimation Strategies

  • • Rounding: Round numbers to make calculations easier
  • • Benchmark numbers: Use 10, 100, 1000 for comparisons
  • • Front-end estimation: Use leading digits only
  • • Compatible numbers: Find numbers that work well together
Example:

Estimate: 487 × 23

Round: 500 × 20 = 10,000
Actual: 11,201 ← Our estimate is reasonable!

Evaluating Reasonableness

Always ask: "Does my answer make sense?"

  • • Check units (miles? dollars? people?)
  • • Check order of magnitude (thousands? millions?)
  • • Compare to known values

Problem Solving: Processes and Techniques

Polya's 4-step problem-solving method and strategies

20-25 min

Polya's 4-Step Problem-Solving Process

  1. 1. Understand the Problem
    • • What are you solving for?
    • • What information do you have?
    • • What do you need to find?
  2. 2. Devise a Plan
    • • What method will you use?
    • • Have you seen a similar problem?
    • • Can you draw a diagram or make a table?
  3. 3. Carry Out the Plan
    • • Execute your chosen strategy
    • • Show your work clearly
    • • Check each step as you go
  4. 4. Look Back and Check
    • • Does your answer make sense?
    • • Did you answer what was asked?
    • • Can you verify your solution?

Problem-Solving Strategies

  • • Work backwards: Start from the desired result and reverse the process
  • • Make a table: Organize information systematically
  • • Find a pattern: Look for recurring relationships
  • • Draw a diagram: Visualize the problem
  • • Guess and check: Try values and refine your approach
  • • Break into smaller problems: Solve parts, then combine

Key Takeaways

✓ Deductive Reasoning: General principles → Specific conclusions (certain)
✓ Inductive Reasoning: Observations → General predictions (probable)
✓ Estimation: Use rounding and benchmarks for quick approximations
✓ Polya's Process: Understand → Plan → Execute → Check

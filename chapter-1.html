1.1 Problem Solving Strategies

Learn systematic approaches to tackling mathematical problems

The Four-Step Problem Solving Process

1
Understand the Problem

Read carefully, identify what you know and what you need to find

2
Devise a Plan

Choose a strategy: draw a picture, make a table, look for patterns

3
Carry Out the Plan

Execute your strategy step by step, show your work

4
Look Back

Check your answer, does it make sense?

Example: The Parking Lot Problem

"A parking lot has 5 rows with 12 spaces in each row. If 3/4 of the spaces are filled, how many cars are in the parking lot?"

1
Understand: 5 rows, 12 spaces each, 3/4 filled. Find: number of cars.
2
Plan: Find total spaces, then find 3/4 of that number.
3
Solve: 5 × 12 = 60 total spaces. 60 × 3/4 = 60 × 0.75 = 45 cars.
4
Check: 45 is 3/4 of 60 ✓. Answer makes sense.
Practice Problems

Problem 1:

A recipe calls for 2 cups of flour to make 12 cookies. How many cups of flour do you need to make 30 cookies?

Setup: 2 cups / 12 cookies = x cups / 30 cookies

Cross multiply: 2 × 30 = 12 × x

Solve: 60 = 12x, so x = 5

Answer: 5 cups of flour

Problem 2:

Look at this pattern: 2, 4, 8, 16, ___. What comes next and why?

Pattern: Each number doubles the previous number

Reasoning: 2 × 2 = 4, 4 × 2 = 8, 8 × 2 = 16

Answer: 32 (because 16 × 2 = 32)

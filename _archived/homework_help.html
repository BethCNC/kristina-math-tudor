Chapter 1: Mathematical Thinking

Problem 1: Deductive vs Inductive Reasoning

Question: Taylor plays football and has caught 24 out of 56 targets so far this season. So, he is expected to catch 6 out of 14 targets in tonight's game.

What type of reasoning is this?

Solution:

This is inductive reasoning.

Explanation: Taylor is making a prediction based on past performance (24/56 = 42.9% success rate). He's using this pattern to predict future performance (6/14 = 42.9%). This is inductive because he's drawing a general conclusion from specific observations.

Problem 2: Pattern Recognition

Question: Find the next two terms in the sequence: 288, 144, 72, 36, ...

Solution:

The next two terms are 18 and 9.

Explanation: This is a geometric sequence where each term is divided by 2 to get the next term.

288 ÷ 2 = 144
144 ÷ 2 = 72
72 ÷ 2 = 36
36 ÷ 2 = 18
18 ÷ 2 = 9

Problem 3: Polya's Problem Solving

Question: A rectangular garden is 12 feet longer than it is wide. The perimeter is 76 feet. Find the dimensions of the garden.

Solution using Polya's 4 steps:

1. Understand: We need to find width and length of a rectangle where length = width + 12, and perimeter = 76.

2. Plan: Use perimeter formula P = 2L + 2W and substitute L = W + 12.

3. Execute:

76 = 2(W + 12) + 2W
76 = 2W + 24 + 2W
76 = 4W + 24
52 = 4W
W = 13 feet
L = 13 + 12 = 25 feet

4. Look back: Check: 2(25) + 2(13) = 50 + 26 = 76 ✓

Chapter 4: Proportions & Percentages

Problem 1: Cross-Multiplication

Question: Solve the proportion: 3/5 = x/20

Solution:

x = 12

3/5 = x/20
3 × 20 = 5 × x (cross-multiplication)
60 = 5x
x = 60 ÷ 5 = 12

Problem 2: Percentage Increase

Question: A shirt costs $40. If the price increases by 15%, what is the new price?

Solution:

New price = $46

Method 1: Direct calculation
New = Original × (1 + rate)
New = $40 × (1 + 0.15)
New = $40 × 1.15 = $46

Method 2: Calculate increase then add
Increase = $40 × 0.15 = $6
New price = $40 + $6 = $46

Problem 3: Unit Rate

Question: A car travels 240 miles in 4 hours. What is the unit rate in miles per hour?

Solution:

Unit rate = 60 miles per hour

Unit Rate = Total Amount ÷ Total Units
Unit Rate = 240 miles ÷ 4 hours
Unit Rate = 60 miles/hour

Question: Calculate the simple interest on $2,000 at 5% for 3 years.

Solution:

I = $2,000 × 0.15

Problem 2: Compound Interest

Question: $1,000 is invested at 6% compounded annually for 5 years. Find the future value.

Solution:

Future Value = $1,338.23

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)
A = $1,000(1 + 0.06/1)^(1×5)
A = $1,000(1.06)^5
A = $1,000 × 1.33823
A = $1,338.23

Problem 3: Rule of 72

Question: How long will it take for money to double at 8% interest?

Solution:

Time to double = 9 years

Years to double = 72 ÷ interest rate
Years to double = 72 ÷ 8
Years to double = 9 years
Chapter 10: Probability

Problem 1: Basic Probability

Question: A bag contains 3 red marbles, 4 blue marbles, and 5 green marbles. What is the probability of drawing a red marble?

Solution:

P(red) = 1/4 or 25%

P(event) = favorable outcomes ÷ total outcomes
P(red) = 3 ÷ (3 + 4 + 5)
P(red) = 3 ÷ 12 = 1/4 = 0.25 = 25%

Problem 2: Complement Rule

Question: If the probability of rain tomorrow is 30%, what is the probability of no rain?

Solution:

P(no rain) = 70%

P(not A) = 1 - P(A)
P(no rain) = 1 - P(rain)
P(no rain) = 1 - 0.30 = 0.70 = 70%

Problem 1: Mean Calculation

Question: Find the mean of the data set: 12, 15, 18, 20, 25

Solution:

Mean = 18

Mean = Sum of values ÷ Number of values
Mean = (12 + 15 + 18 + 20 + 25) ÷ 5
Mean = 90 ÷ 5 = 18

Chapter 5: Linear & Exponential Functions

Problem 1: Slope Calculation

Question: Find the slope between points (2, 5) and (6, 13)

Solution:

Slope = 2

Slope = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁)
Slope = (13-5)/(6-2)
Slope = 8/4 = 2

Question: Convert 2.5 miles to feet

Chapter 13: Voting & Apportionment

Problem 1: Plurality Method

Question: In an election with 3 candidates, the votes are: A gets 45, B gets 38, C gets 17. Who wins by plurality?

Solution:

Candidate A wins by plurality

Total votes: 45 + 38 + 17 = 100
A: 45 votes (45%)
B: 38 votes (38%)
C: 17 votes (17%)
A has the most votes, so A wins by plurality

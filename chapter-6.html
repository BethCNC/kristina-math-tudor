Key Formulas for Test 3

Simple Interest

I = P × r × t

I = Interest earned, P = Principal (initial amount), r = annual interest rate (as decimal), t = time in years

Example: $1000 at 5% for 2 years = $1000 × 0.05 × 2 = $100

Compound Interest

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

A = Final amount, P = Principal, r = annual rate, n = compounding periods per year, t = time in years

Example: $1000 at 5% compounded quarterly for 2 years = $1000(1 + 0.05/4)^(4×2) = $1104.49

Monthly Payment (Loan)

PMT = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1]

PMT = Monthly payment, P = Principal, r = monthly interest rate, n = number of payments

Example: $20,000 car loan at 6% for 5 years (60 payments) ≈ $386.66/month

Practice Problems for Test 3

Problem 1: Simple Interest

You deposit $2,500 in a savings account that earns 3.5% simple interest per year. How much interest will you earn after 4 years?

Solution:

I = P × r × t = $2,500 × 0.035 × 4 = $350

Problem 2: Compound Interest

You invest $5,000 at 4% interest compounded monthly for 3 years. What is the final amount?

Solution:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) = $5,000(1 + 0.04/12)^(12×3) = $5,000(1.00333)^36 ≈ $5,636.36

Problem 3: Monthly Payment

You take out a $15,000 loan at 6% annual interest for 4 years. What is your monthly payment?

Solution:

r = 0.06/12 = 0.005, n = 4×12 = 48

PMT = $15,000 × [0.005(1.005)^48] / [(1.005)^48 - 1] ≈ $352.28

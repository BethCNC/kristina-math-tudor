üìä Understanding Ratios

What is a Ratio?

A comparison of two or more quantities

Written as: 3:4, 3 to 4, or 3/4

Example: 3 cups flour to 4 cups water

Simplifying Ratios

Divide both numbers by their GCD

Example: 12:16

GCD = 4, so 12:16 = 3:4

‚öñÔ∏è Solving Proportions

Step-by-Step Method

1. Set up the proportion: a/b = c/d

2. Cross multiply: a √ó d = b √ó c

3. Solve for the unknown variable

4. Check your answer by substituting back

üçé Real Example: Grocery Shopping

Problem: 3 apples cost $2. How much do 9 apples cost?

Set up: 3/2 = 9/x

Cross multiply: 3x = 18

Solve: x = 6

Answer: 9 apples cost $6

üíØ Working with Percentages

Finding %

Part √∑ Whole √ó 100

15 out of 60 = 25%

Finding Part

% √ó Whole √∑ 100

25% of 60 = 15

Finding Whole

Part √∑ % √ó 100

15 is 25% of 60

üß† Practice Problems

Problem 1: Recipe Scaling

A recipe calls for 2 cups flour to serve 4 people. How much flour is needed to serve 10 people?

Show Solution

Set up proportion: 2/4 = x/10

Cross multiply: 2 √ó 10 = 4 √ó x

Solve: 20 = 4x ‚Üí x = 5

Answer: 5 cups flour needed

Problem 2: Test Score

You got 42 questions right out of 60 total. What percentage did you score?

Show Solution

Formula: Part/Whole √ó 100

Calculate: 42/60 √ó 100

Simplify: 0.7 √ó 100 = 70

Answer: 70% score

Problem 3: Shopping Discount

A shirt costs $45 and is on sale for 20% off. What is the sale price?

Show Solution

Discount amount: 20% of $45 = 0.20 √ó 45 = $9

Sale price: $45 - $9 = $36

Alternative: 80% of $45 = 0.80 √ó 45 = $36

Answer: $36 sale price

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Wrong Setup

Make sure units match on both sides of the proportion

‚ùå Decimal Places

When converting decimals to percentages, remember to multiply by 100

‚ùå Cross Multiplication

Multiply diagonally: a/b = c/d becomes ad = bc

‚ùå Not Checking

Always substitute your answer back to verify it works

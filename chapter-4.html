4.1 Basic Proportions

Learn to set up and solve proportion equations

What is a Proportion?

A proportion is a statement that two ratios are equal.

If a/b = c/d, then we can cross multiply: a × d = b × c

Setting Up Proportions
1 Identify the two ratios
2 Set them equal to each other
3 Cross multiply
4 Solve for the unknown
Example Problem

Problem: If 3 apples cost $2, how much do 12 apples cost?

Setup: 3 apples / $2 = 12 apples / $x

Cross multiply: 3x = 2 × 12

Solve: 3x = 24, so x = $8

Answer: 12 apples cost $8

Practice Problems

Problem 1:

A car travels 240 miles on 8 gallons of gas. At this rate, how many gallons would be needed to travel 420 miles?

Setup: 240 miles / 8 gallons = 420 miles / x gallons

Cross multiply: 240x = 8 × 420

Solve: 240x = 3,360, so x = 14

Answer: 14 gallons

Problem 2:

A store is having a 25% off sale. If a jacket originally costs $80, what is the sale price?

Find discount: 25% of $80 = 0.25 × 80 = $20

Subtract from original: $80 - $20 = $60

Alternative method: $80 × (1 - 0.25) = $80 × 0.75 = $60

Answer: $60

4.2 Percentages

Master percentage calculations and applications

Find the Part

What is 25% of 80?

0.25 × 80 = 20

Find the Whole

20 is 25% of what number?

20 ÷ 0.25 = 80

Find the Percent

20 is what % of 80?

20 ÷ 80 = 0.25 = 25%

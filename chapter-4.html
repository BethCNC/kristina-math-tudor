4.1

Proportions, Percentages, and Ratios

Foundation concepts for working with proportional relationships

Key Concepts

Ratios

Compare two quantities:

3:4 or 3/4

Example: 3 cups flour to 4 cups water

Proportions

Two equal ratios:

a/b = c/d

Cross-multiply: ad = bc

Percentages

Parts per hundred:

25% = 25/100 = 0.25

Formula: P% × N = 0.P × N

Worked Example: Setting Up Proportions

Problem: If 3 pencils cost $2.40, how much do 8 pencils cost?

1

Set up the proportion:

3 pencils / $2.40 = 8 pencils / x

2

Cross-multiply:

3x = 8 × $2.40 = $19.20

3

Solve for x:

x = $19.20 ÷ 3 = $6.40

Answer: 8 pencils cost $6.40

4.2

Using Percentages

Calculate percentage increases, decreases, and find original amounts

Essential Formulas

Percentage of a Number

P% × N = (P/100) × N

Example: 25% of 80 = 0.25 × 80 = 20

Percentage Change

% Change = (New - Old) / Old × 100%

Positive = increase, Negative = decrease

Common Applications

Price Increases

New Price = Original × (1 + rate)

Discounts

Sale Price = Original × (1 - rate)

Tax Calculations

Total = Price × (1 + tax rate)

4.3

Rates and Unit Rates

Compare quantities with different units and find the best deals

What are Rates?

A rate compares two different units, like miles per hour or price per pound. A unit rate has a denominator of 1.

Common Rate Examples

Speed: 60 miles per hour

Distance ÷ Time = Rate

Unit Price: $2.50 per pound

Total Cost ÷ Amount = Unit Price

Efficiency: 30 miles per gallon

Distance ÷ Fuel = Efficiency

Comparison Shopping

Which is the better deal?

Option A: 12 oz for $3.60

Option B: 16 oz for $4.40

Solution:

A: $3.60 ÷ 12 oz = $0.30 per oz

B: $4.40 ÷ 16 oz = $0.275 per oz

Option B is cheaper!

4.4

Dimensional Analysis

Convert between different units using conversion factors

The Strategy: Cancel Units

Example: Convert 2.5 hours to minutes

2.5 hours × (60 minutes / 1 hour) = 150 minutes

↑ Hours cancel out ↑

Key Rules:

  • • Write conversions as fractions
  • • Units should cancel out
  • • Check your final units

Common Conversions:

  • • 1 hour = 60 minutes
  • • 1 foot = 12 inches
  • • 1 yard = 3 feet

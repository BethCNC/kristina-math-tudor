4.1

Proportions, Percentages, and Ratios

Foundation concepts for working with proportional relationships

Key Concepts (Think Like a 5th Grader!)

Ratios

What it is: Comparing two quantities (like recipes!)

Examples:

  • • 3:4 or 3/4 (3 cups flour to 4 cups water)
  • • 2:1 (2 boys for every 1 girl)
  • • 5:2 (5 red marbles to 2 blue marbles)

Proportions

What it is: Two equal ratios (like equivalent fractions!)

Formula:

a/b = c/d

Cross-multiply:

ad = bc

Percentages

What it is: Parts per hundred (like grades and sales!)

Examples:

  • • 25% = 25/100 = 0.25
  • • 50% = 50/100 = 0.5
  • • 100% = 100/100 = 1.0

Cross-Multiplication

What it is: The magic trick to solve proportions!

Rule:

If a/b = c/d, then ad = bc

Example:

If 3/4 = x/12, then 3×12 = 4×x

Study Tips & Tricks

1

Set up proportions carefully - make sure units match!

2

Cross-multiply to solve - it's like magic!

3

Convert between formats - fractions, decimals, and percentages

Worked Example: The Recipe Problem

Problem: A recipe calls for 2 cups of flour to make 12 cookies. How many cups of flour do you need to make 36 cookies?

Step 1: Set up the proportion

2 cups flour / 12 cookies = x cups flour / 36 cookies

2/12 = x/36

Step 2: Cross-multiply

2 × 36 = 12 × x

72 = 12x

Step 3: Solve for x

72 ÷ 12 = x

6 = x

Step 4: Check your answer

• 2 cups for 12 cookies = 1/6 cup per cookie

• 6 cups for 36 cookies = 1/6 cup per cookie ✓

Answer: 6 cups of flour

Practice Question

Question: If 3 apples cost $2.25, how much do 8 apples cost?

Cross-multiply: 3x = 2.25 × 8 = 18

Problem: If 3 pencils cost $2.40, how much do 8 pencils cost?

1

Set up the proportion:

3 pencils / $2.40 = 8 pencils / x

2

Cross-multiply:

3x = 8 × $2.40 = $19.20

3

Solve for x:

x = $19.20 ÷ 3 = $6.40

Answer: 8 pencils cost $6.40

4.2

Using Percentages

Calculate percentage increases, decreases, and find original amounts

Key Concepts (Think Like a 5th Grader!)

Percentage of a Number

What it is: Finding a part of a whole (like a slice of pizza!)

Formula:

P% × N = 0.P × N

Example:

25% of 80 = 0.25 × 80 = 20

Percentage Increase/Decrease

What it is: How much something goes up or down (like prices!)

Increase:

New = Original × (1 + rate)

Decrease:

New = Original × (1 - rate)

Finding Original Amount

What it is: Working backwards to find the starting amount!

Formula:

Original = New ÷ (1 ± rate)

Example:

If $90 is 10% off, original = $90 ÷ 0.9 = $100

Percentage Change

What it is: How much something changed as a percentage!

Formula:

% Change = (New - Old) / Old × 100%

Example:

$50 to $60 = (60-50)/50 × 100% = 20% increase

Study Tips & Tricks

1

Increase: New = Original × (1 + rate)

2

Decrease: New = Original × (1 - rate)

3

To find original: Original = New ÷ (1 ± rate)

Worked Example: Sale Price Problem

Problem: A shirt costs $40. It's on sale for 25% off. What's the sale price?

Step 1: Identify the values

Original = $40, Rate = 25% = 0.25

Step 2: Use decrease formula

New = Original × (1 - rate)

New = $40 × (1 - 0.25)

New = $40 × 0.75

Step 3: Calculate

New = $30

Step 4: Check your answer

• 25% off means you pay 75% of original

• 75% of $40 = $30 ✓

Answer: $30

Practice Question

Question: If a $60 item is marked up 15%, what's the new price?

Click to see answer

Use increase formula: New = Original × (1 + rate)

Calculate: New = $60 × (1 + 0.15) = $60 × 1.15 = $69

Answer: $69

4.3

Rates and Unit Rates

Compare quantities with different units and find the best deals

Key Concepts (Think Like a 5th Grader!)

Rate

What it is: Comparing two different units (like speed!)

Examples:

  • • 60 miles per hour
  • • $2.50 per pound
  • • 3 cookies per minute

Unit Rate

What it is: Rate with denominator of 1 (per unit!)

Formula:

Unit rate = Total amount ÷ Total units

Example:

$10 for 4 apples = $2.50 per apple

Rate of Change

What it is: How one quantity changes relative to another!

Examples:

  • • Temperature change per hour
  • • Population growth per year
  • • Price increase per month

Comparing Rates

What it is: Finding the best deal by comparing rates!

Strategy:

• Convert both to same unit rate

• Compare the numbers

• Lower price per unit = better deal

Study Tips & Tricks

1

Unit rate = Total amount ÷ Total units

2

Use rates to compare different options

3

Identify which rate is better for decision making

Worked Example: Best Deal Problem

Problem: Which is the better deal: 12 apples for $8 or 8 apples for $6?

Step 1: Find unit rate for first option

$8 ÷ 12 apples = $0.67 per apple

Step 2: Find unit rate for second option

$6 ÷ 8 apples = $0.75 per apple

Step 3: Compare the rates

$0.67 vs $0.75 per apple

Step 4: Choose the better deal

• $0.67 is less than $0.75

• Lower price per apple = better deal ✓

Answer: 12 apples for $8 is the better deal

Practice Question

Question: If you drive 240 miles in 4 hours, what's your speed in miles per hour?

Click to see answer

Use unit rate formula: Unit rate = Total amount ÷ Total units

Calculate: Speed = 240 miles ÷ 4 hours = 60 miles per hour

Answer: 60 mph

Common Rate Examples

Speed: 60 miles per hour

Distance ÷ Time = Rate

Unit Price: $2.50 per pound

Total Cost ÷ Amount = Unit Price

Efficiency: 30 miles per gallon

Distance ÷ Fuel = Efficiency

Comparison Shopping

Which is the better deal?

Option A: 12 oz for $3.60

Option B: 16 oz for $4.40

Solution:

A: $3.60 ÷ 12 oz = $0.30 per oz

B: $4.40 ÷ 16 oz = $0.275 per oz

Option B is cheaper!

4.4

Dimensional Analysis

Convert between different units using conversion factors

Key Concepts (Think Like a 5th Grader!)

Converting Between Units

What it is: Changing from one unit to another (like recipes!)

Strategy:

• Write conversions as fractions

• Set up so units cancel

• Chain multiple conversions

Conversion Factors

What it is: Fractions that equal 1 (like 60 min/1 hour!)

Examples:

  • • 60 minutes / 1 hour
  • • 12 inches / 1 foot
  • • 3 feet / 1 yard

Multi-step Conversions

What it is: Converting through multiple steps!

Example:

Days → Hours → Minutes

Miles → Feet → Inches

Checking Units

What it is: Always check your final units!

Rule:

• Final units should match what's asked

• If units don't match, check your setup

Study Tips & Tricks

1

Write conversions as fractions so units cancel

2

Chain multiple conversions together

3

Always check final units match what's asked

Worked Example: Time Conversion

Problem: Convert 2.5 hours to minutes

Step 1: Set up conversion factor

2.5 hours × (60 minutes / 1 hour)

Step 2: Cancel units

2.5 × 60 minutes = 150 minutes

↑ Hours cancel out ↑

Step 3: Check final units

Final answer: 150 minutes ✓

Practice Question

Question: Convert 3 feet to inches

Click to see answer

Set up conversion: 3 feet × (12 inches / 1 foot)

Calculate: 3 × 12 inches = 36 inches

Answer: 36 inches

Personal Finance Formulas (Chapter 6)

Simple Interest

I = Prt

  • I = Interest earned
  • P = Principal (starting amount)
  • r = annual interest rate (as decimal)
  • t = time in years

Compound Interest

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

  • A = Final amount
  • P = Principal
  • r = annual interest rate (as decimal)
  • n = compounding periods per year
  • t = time in years

Compounding Frequencies:
Annually: n=1, Quarterly: n=4, Monthly: n=12, Daily: n=365

Continuous Compound Interest

A = Pe^(rt)

Annual Percentage Yield (APY)

APY = ((1 + r/n)^n - 1) × 100%

Present Value

PV = A / (1 + r/n)^(nt)

Annuity Future Value

FV = PMT × [((1 + r/n)^(nt) - 1) / (r/n)]

Fixed Installment Loan Payment

PMT = (P × r/n) / (1 - (1 + r/n)^(-nt))

Credit Card Debt Payments

R = -log(1 - (r/n)(A/PMT)) / log(1 + r/n)

Measurement & Conversions (Chapter 7)

US Customary System

Length

  • 12 inches = 1 foot
  • 3 feet = 1 yard
  • 5280 feet = 1 mile

Mass

  • 16 ounces = 1 pound
  • 2000 pounds = 1 ton

Capacity

  • 8 fluid ounces = 1 cup
  • 2 cups = 1 pint
  • 2 pints = 1 quart
  • 4 quarts = 1 gallon

Metric System Prefixes

  • Kilo- (1000)
  • Hecto- (100)
  • Deka- (10)
  • Base Unit (1)
  • Deci- (0.1)
  • Centi- (0.01)
  • Milli- (0.001)

Key US to Metric Conversions

Length

  • 1 inch = 2.54 cm
  • 1 foot ≈ 0.305 m
  • 1 yard ≈ 0.914 m
  • 1 mile ≈ 1.61 km

Mass

  • 1 ounce ≈ 28.35 g
  • 1 pound ≈ 0.454 kg

Temperature Conversions

F = (9C/5) + 32

C = 5(F-32)/9

Statistics & Probability (Chapters 10 & 11)

Z-Score Formula

z = (data - mean) / standard deviation

For Populations:

z = (x - μ) / σ

For Samples:

z = (x - x̄) / s

Empirical Rule (68-95-99.7)

For normally distributed data:

  • 68% of data falls within 1 standard deviation of the mean
  • 95% of data falls within 2 standard deviations of the mean
  • 99.7% of data falls within 3 standard deviations of the mean

Probability

P(event) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes

Expected Value

E(X) = Σ(x × P(x))

Voting Methods & Apportionment (Chapter 13)

Election Counting Methods

Majority Rule

Winner must have more than 50% of the votes

Plurality

Candidate with the most votes wins (no majority required)

Borda Count

Points assigned based on ranking positions

Plurality with Elimination

Runoff elections until majority is achieved

Pairwise Comparison

Head-to-head matchups: n(n-1)/2 comparisons

Apportionment

Standard Divisor (SD)

SD = total population / total seats

Round to 4 decimal places

Standard Quota (SQ)

SQ = state population / SD

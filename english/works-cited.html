Works Cited Guide

Works Cited Page Setup

  • • Start on a new page at the end of your paper
  • • Center the title "Works Cited" (not bolded or underlined)
  • • List all sources in alphabetical order by first word
  • • Use hanging indent (first line flush left, others indented 1/2 inch)
  • • Double-space everything (no extra spaces between entries)
  • • Continue page numbers from body of paper

Book (Print)

Format:

Author Last Name, First Name. Book Title. Publisher, Year.

Example:

Gladwell, Malcolm. Outliers: The Story of Success. Little, Brown and Company, 2008.

Article from Database

Format:

Author. "Article Title." Journal Name, vol. #, no. #, Year, pp. ##-##. Database Name, DOI or URL.

Example:

Smith, John. "The Impact of Social Media on Teens." Journal of Adolescent Health, vol. 45, no. 2, 2020, pp. 123-135. EBSCO, doi:10.1234/jah.2020.45.2.123.

Website

Format:

Author. "Page Title." Website Name, Publisher/Sponsor, Date, URL. Accessed Date.

Example:

Grant, Adam. "Does Trying to Be Happy Make Us Unhappy?" Psychology Today, Sussex Publishers, 15 May 2020, www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/give-and-take/202005/does-trying-be-happy-make-us-unhappy. Accessed 10 Oct. 2025.

Source with No Author

Start with the title, then continue with the rest of the citation:

"The Benefits of Exercise." Health Magazine, vol. 12, no. 3, 2021, pp. 45-50.

Multiple Authors

Two Authors:

Smith, John, and Jane Doe. Title. Publisher, Year.

Three or More Authors:

Smith, John, et al. Title. Publisher, Year.

Note: "et al." means "and others"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Forgetting hanging indent

Second and subsequent lines must be indented 1/2 inch

❌ Not alphabetizing

Entries must be in alphabetical order by first word

❌ Wrong punctuation

Use periods between major elements, commas within elements

❌ Italics vs. quotes

Use italics for longer works (books, journals), quotes for shorter works (articles, chapters)

Essay 4

Research Paper

Due Date: Monday, December 8, 2025 at 11:59 PM

Final essay of the semester - submit via Brightspace (D2L).

Assignment Overview

This research paper is your opportunity to explore a topic in depth using academic research. You'll develop an argumentative thesis and support it with evidence from multiple credible sources.

Length

1500-2000 words

Format

MLA 9th Edition

Sources

Minimum 5 credible sources

Requirements

Topic & Thesis

  • • Choose a topic appropriate for academic research
  • • Develop an arguable thesis (not just a statement of fact)
  • • Topic should allow for multiple perspectives and in-depth analysis

Research & Sources

  • • Minimum 5 credible sources required
  • • Use academic journals, books, and reputable websites
  • • Avoid Wikipedia, personal blogs, and unreliable sources
  • • Use CPCC library databases for peer-reviewed sources
  • • Evaluate source credibility and relevance

Format & Citations

  • • MLA 9th Edition format throughout
  • • Proper heading (name, instructor, course, date)
  • • Header with last name and page number
  • • In-text citations for all quotes and paraphrases
  • • Works Cited page with all sources (alphabetical order)

Research Process

Step 1: Choose & Narrow Your Topic

Start broad, then narrow to something specific and manageable.

Example:
Too broad: "Social media"
Better: "The impact of social media on teenage mental health"
Best: "How Instagram's algorithm affects body image in female teens aged 13-17"

Step 2: Conduct Preliminary Research

Explore your topic to see what research exists and refine your focus.

  • • Use CPCC library databases (EBSCO, JSTOR, etc.)
  • • Look for recent, peer-reviewed sources
  • • Take detailed notes with full citation info

Step 3: Develop Your Thesis

Based on your research, craft an arguable thesis statement.

Strong thesis example:
"While social media companies claim their algorithms promote user engagement, research shows these systems disproportionately harm teenage girls by prioritizing appearance-focused content that exacerbates body image issues."

Step 4: Create Detailed Outline

Organize your argument and evidence before drafting.

Step 5: Write, Revise, Edit

Draft your essay, then revise multiple times before submitting.

Critical Thinking for Writing

Develop analytical skills for Essay 3 & 4

What is Critical Thinking?

Critical thinking in writing means analyzing information deeply, questioning assumptions, and forming well-supported arguments. It's essential for Essays 3 and 4.

Analyze

Break down ideas into parts, examine relationships

Evaluate

Judge credibility, relevance, and strength of evidence

Synthesize

Combine ideas to create new understanding

Argue

Present well-reasoned, evidence-based claims

Critical Reading Process

1. Preview the Text

Skim titles, headings, introduction, and conclusion to understand main idea.

2. Read Actively

Annotate, highlight key points, question assumptions, note connections.

3. Analyze Arguments

Identify claims, evidence, assumptions. Evaluate strength and validity.

4. Respond and Reflect

Develop your own perspective based on your analysis.

Critical Thinking

What is Critical Thinking?

Critical thinking is the ability to analyze information, evaluate evidence, and form reasoned judgments. In writing, it means going beyond summary to ask deeper questions about meaning, significance, and implications.

Key principle: Don't just describe what something says—analyze WHY it matters and HOW it works.

Summary vs. Analysis

❌ Summary (Not Enough)

Retelling what the text says:

"Smith says that social media affects teenagers negatively."

✅ Analysis (Good!)

Explaining significance and implications:

"Smith's argument reveals how algorithm design prioritizes profit over user wellbeing, raising ethical questions about corporate responsibility."

Critical Thinking Questions

Ask yourself these questions while reading and writing:

What is the main argument?

Identify the author's central claim

What evidence supports it?

Evaluate the strength and credibility of sources

What assumptions are being made?

Identify unstated beliefs the argument relies on

What are the implications?

Consider "So what?" and "What does this mean?"

Are there alternative perspectives?

Consider counterarguments and other viewpoints

How does this connect to broader contexts?

Link to larger social, cultural, or academic conversations

Analytical Sentence Stems

Use these phrases to move beyond summary:

"This suggests that..."
"The significance of this is..."
"This reveals a tension between..."
"While [author] argues X, this overlooks..."
"This raises questions about..."
"The implications of this extend to..."

Critical Reading Strategies

  • Annotate as you read

    Mark confusing passages, interesting claims, and questions

  • Question the author

    Don't accept claims at face value—ask for evidence

  • Make connections

    Link to other readings, personal experience, or current events

  • Identify patterns

    Notice repeated ideas, motifs, or rhetorical strategies

  • Consider context

    Think about when, where, and why the text was written

Signs You Need More Critical Thinking

⚠️ Your essay mostly summarizes what sources say
⚠️ You don't explain WHY evidence matters
⚠️ Every paragraph starts with "The author says..."
⚠️ You don't consider counterarguments or complexity
⚠️ Your conclusion just repeats your intro

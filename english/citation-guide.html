MLA In-Text Citations

Quick reference guide for citing sources in your essays

Basic Format

(Author Last Name Page Number)

In-text citations briefly identify sources within your paper and correspond to full entries on your Works Cited page.

Common Citation Examples

1. Author + Page Number

(Smith 42).

Note: No comma between author and page number

2. Author in Signal Phrase

Smith argues that "quotation here" (42).

When you name the author in your sentence, only include page number

3. No Author (Use Title)

("Article Title" 15).

Use shortened title if no author is listed

4. Website (No Page Number)

(Jones).

Online sources without page numbers only need author name

5. Two Authors

(Smith and Jones 25).

Use "and" between two authors

6. Three or More Authors

(Smith et al. 33).

Use first author's last name followed by "et al."

Important Rules

  • Citation goes BEFORE the period at the end of the sentence
  • No comma between author name and page number
  • If quoting, the citation goes AFTER the closing quotation mark
  • Every in-text citation must have a matching Works Cited entry
  • Use the same name/title format as your Works Cited entry

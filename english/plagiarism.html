Avoiding Plagiarism

Critical information for academic integrity

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is using someone else's words, ideas, or work without giving proper credit. It includes:

Direct Plagiarism

Copying text word-for-word without quotation marks or citation

Paraphrasing Plagiarism

Rewording someone's ideas without citation

Self-Plagiarism

Submitting your own previous work without permission

Accidental Plagiarism

Forgetting to cite or citing incorrectly

How to Avoid Plagiarism

1. Always Cite Your Sources

Include in-text citations and Works Cited entries for ALL sources, including:

  • Direct quotes
  • Paraphrased ideas
  • Statistics and data
  • Images and graphics

2. Use Quotation Marks

When using exact words from a source, put them in quotation marks AND cite the source.

3. Paraphrase Correctly

Rewrite ideas in your own words AND style. Simply changing a few words is NOT paraphrasing. You must still cite paraphrased content.

4. Keep Track of Sources

As you research, immediately note all source information. Don't wait until the end to find citations.

Consequences of Plagiarism

Plagiarism is a serious academic offense at CPCC that can result in:

  • Failing grade on the assignment
  • Failing grade in the course
  • Academic probation
  • Suspension or expulsion from college

MLA Basics

MLA Page Setup

Margins & Spacing

  • • 1-inch margins on all sides (top, bottom, left, right)
  • • Double-spaced throughout entire paper (no extra spaces)
  • • 12-point font in a readable typeface (Times New Roman, Arial, Calibri)

Header (Top Right Corner)

Your last name followed by page number (automatically generated):

Smith 1

Heading (Top Left, First Page Only)

Four lines in this exact order:

Your Name Instructor Name ENG 111 N815 16 October 2025

Note: Use day-month-year format

Title

  • • Centered on the line after your heading
  • • Same font size as body text (12 point)
  • • Not bolded, underlined, or in ALL CAPS
  • • Capitalize major words (not articles, prepositions, conjunctions unless first/last word)

In-Text Citations (Parenthetical)

MLA uses parenthetical citations with author's last name and page number.

Basic Format

Author's last name + page number (no comma):

(Smith 42).

Author Named in Sentence

If you mention the author in your sentence, only include page number:

According to Smith, "happiness is subjective" (42).

No Page Number (Web Sources)

For websites without page numbers, use author name only:

(Grant).

No Author

Use shortened title (in quotes for articles, italics for books):

("Impact of Social Media" 5).

Using Quotations

Short Quotations (< 4 lines)

Integrate into your paragraph with quotation marks:

Grant argues that "pursuing happiness directly often backfires" (12).

Long Quotations (≥ 4 lines)

Block format: indent 1/2 inch, no quotation marks, citation after period:

Grant explains the paradox of happiness: When people pursue happiness directly, they often become more focused on whether they're happy, which ironically makes them less happy. (12)

Adding to or Omitting from Quotations

  • • Brackets [like this]: Add clarifying words
  • • Ellipsis ...: Omit unnecessary words
  • • Example: "The study [conducted in 2020] revealed..."

Works Cited Page Basics

  • • New page at end of paper
  • • Title "Works Cited" centered at top (not bolded)
  • • Alphabetical order by first word (usually author's last name)
  • • Hanging indent: First line flush left, subsequent lines indented 1/2 inch
  • • Double-spaced throughout, no extra spaces between entries

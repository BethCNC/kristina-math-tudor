NOTES
One of the most common types of essays you will be asked to write in college are compare/contrast essays. Usually you will have to write these types of essays in psychology, sociology or even history. Most of you have written a compare/contrast before so my notes will be brief.

A comparison shows how two or more things are alike, and of course a contrast shows how they are different. One thing to keep in mind is that you don't necessarily have to do both within an essay. Sometimes you will only be asked to compare, other times you will only be asked to contrast, and again other times you will be asked to do both. Make sure it is clear to you what you are being asked to do!

Your main objective within an essay is to clarify and explain the similarities/differences/both. However, no one wants to read a compare/contrast essay that only points out the obvious. Always force yourself to go beyond the surface similarities/differences. Even challenge yourself to show that two things which seem alike are actually different and vice versa.

In order to make this information clear, you need to organize the essay so that is flows smoothly. You can use the subject-by-subject method or the point-by-point method.

Subject-By-Subject

Give all the information about topic A and then give the same information in the same order about topic B:

First All Topic  A: Point 1, Point 2, Point 3

Then All Topic B: Point 1, Point 2, Point 3

 

Point by Point

  • Move back and forth between A and B. 
  • Give one point about A and then go to the parallel point about B. 
  • Then move to the next point for A and do the same.

                        First A, Point 1;           Then B, Point 1

                        First A, Point 2;           Then B, Point 2

                        First A, Point 3;           Then B, Point 3

 

You also need to make sure you use appropriate transitions to show comparisons and/or contrasts:

For Contrast:   Use change-of-direction transition words like on the other hand, however, instead, alternatively, although, whereas, but, however, conversely, in contrast, while, yet, unlike, despite, different from, except.

 

For Comparison:  Use illustration or addition transitions such as furthermore, moreover, additionally, and, also, in addition, as well as, both, neither, similarly, like, too, the same, again, too, equally.

 

Make sure to look at the sample student essays. These students earned an A on their essays because it was very interesting to read, had few mistakes, flowed well and really took a hard look at the similarities/differences in the topic.

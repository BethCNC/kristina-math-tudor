Course Overview

Course Information

Course Details:

  • • ENG 111: Writing & Inquiry
  • • 8 weeks: Aug 18 - Oct 13
  • • Online format
  • • 4 major essays required

Learning Objectives:

  • • Develop critical reading skills
  • • Write clear, organized essays
  • • Use proper citation methods
  • • Avoid plagiarism

Required Essays

Essay Schedule:

  • • Essay 1: Critical Reading Response
  • • Essay 2: Compare/Contrast
  • • Essay 3: Argumentative
  • • Essay 4: Research Paper

Grading:

  • • Essays: 60%
  • • Discussions: 20%
  • • Quizzes: 15%
  • • Participation: 5%
Essay Writing Guides

Essay 1: Critical Reading Response

Requirements:

  • • 300-500 words
  • • Respond to assigned reading
  • • Include thesis statement
  • • Use proper citations

Structure:

  • • Introduction with thesis
  • • 2-3 body paragraphs
  • • Conclusion
  • • Works Cited page

Essay 2: Compare/Contrast

Requirements:

  • • 500-750 words
  • • Compare two subjects
  • • Clear organization
  • • Supporting evidence

Organization Options:

  • • Point-by-point method
  • • Block method
  • • Choose based on topic

Essay 3: Argumentative

Requirements:

  • • 750-1000 words
  • • Clear position statement
  • • Counterarguments
  • • Strong evidence

Structure:

  • • Introduction with claim
  • • Background information
  • • Supporting arguments
  • • Counterarguments & rebuttals
  • • Conclusion

Essay 4: Research Paper

Requirements:

  • • 1000-1250 words
  • • 3-5 credible sources
  • • MLA format
  • • Annotated bibliography

Research Process:

  • • Choose topic
  • • Find sources
  • • Take notes
  • • Write outline
  • • Draft and revise

Critical Reading

Strategies:

  • • Preview the text
  • • Read actively
  • • Take notes
  • • Ask questions
  • • Summarize key points

Citation & Plagiarism

MLA Format:

  • • Author's last name, first name
  • • Title in quotation marks
  • • Title of container
  • • Publisher, date
  • • Page numbers
Discussion Post Guidelines

Writing Discussion Posts

Requirements:

  • • 300 words minimum
  • • Respond to the prompt
  • • Include specific examples
  • • Cite sources when needed
  • • Respond to classmates

Structure:

  • • Clear introduction
  • • Main points with examples
  • • Thoughtful conclusion
  • • Engaging question for others

Responding to Others

Guidelines:

  • • Be respectful and constructive
  • • Build on others' ideas
  • • Ask clarifying questions
  • • Share additional insights
  • • 100-150 words minimum

Avoid:

  • • Personal attacks
  • • "I agree" without substance
  • • Off-topic comments
  • • All caps or excessive punctuation

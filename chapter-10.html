P(not A) = 1 - P(A)

The probability that an event does NOT occur equals 1 minus the probability it does occur.

P(no rain) = 1 - 0.30 = 0.70 or 70%

Odds

Odds in favor: ratio of favorable outcomes to unfavorable outcomes
Odds against: ratio of unfavorable outcomes to favorable outcomes

Example: Drawing a heart from a standard deck of 52 cards

Independent Events (AND)

P(A and B) = P(A) Ã— P(B)

When two events are independent (one doesn't affect the other), multiply their probabilities.

Example: Flipping a coin twice
P(heads on first flip AND heads on second flip) = 1/2 Ã— 1/2 = 1/4 = 0.25

Mutually Exclusive Events (OR)

P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B)

When two events cannot happen at the same time (mutually exclusive), add their probabilities.

Example: Rolling a die
P(rolling a 2 OR rolling a 5) = 1/6 + 1/6 = 2/6 = 1/3

ðŸ’¡ Real-World Applications

Games & Gambling

Casinos use expected value to ensure they profit long-term. Understanding EV helps you make informed betting decisions.

Insurance

Insurance companies calculate expected losses to set premiums. The premium must exceed the expected payout to stay profitable.

Investment Decisions

Investors use expected value to compare risky vs. safe investments and decide which opportunities are worth pursuing.

Medical Testing

Doctors interpret test results using probability to determine the likelihood of disease given symptoms and test accuracy.

10.1 Basic Probability

Understanding and calculating probability

Probability Formula

P(event) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes

• Probability is always between 0 and 1

• P = 0 means impossible, P = 1 means certain

• Can be expressed as fractions, decimals, or percentages

Probability Scale
Impossible P = 0
Unlikely P < 0.5
50-50 P = 0.5
Likely P > 0.5
Certain P = 1
Example: Rolling a Die

What's P(rolling a 4)?

Favorable: 1 way (rolling 4)

Total: 6 possible outcomes

P(4): 1/6 ≈ 0.167 or 16.7%

What's P(even number)?

Favorable: 3 ways (2, 4, 6)

P(even) = 3/6 = 1/2 = 0.5 or 50%

Practice Problems

Problem 1:

A bag contains 5 red balls, 3 blue balls, and 2 green balls. What is the probability of randomly selecting a blue ball?

Total balls: 5 + 3 + 2 = 10 balls

Blue balls: 3

Formula: P(blue) = Number of blue balls / Total balls

Calculate: P(blue) = 3/10 = 0.3 = 30%

Answer: 3/10 or 0.3 or 30%

Problem 2:

You flip a coin and roll a six-sided die. What is the probability of getting heads on the coin AND a 4 on the die?

P(heads): 1/2 = 0.5

P(rolling 4): 1/6 ≈ 0.167

Independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B)

Calculate: P(heads and 4) = (1/2) × (1/6) = 1/12

Answer: 1/12 ≈ 0.083 or 8.3%

10.2 Expected Value

Calculating long-term averages and making decisions

Expected Value Formula

E(X) = Σ(outcome × probability)

• Expected value is the long-term average

• Multiply each outcome by its probability, then add

• Helps compare different options or games

Step-by-Step Process
1 List all possible outcomes
2 Find probability of each outcome
3 Multiply outcome × probability
4 Add all the products
Example: Simple Game

Game costs $2. Win $10 (prob 1/5) or win $0 (prob 4/5)

Outcomes: Win $8 or lose $2 (net amounts)

E(X): $8×(1/5) + (-$2)×(4/5)

E(X): $1.60 + (-$1.60) = $0

Result: Break-even game on average

10.3 Complement Rule

Using "what doesn't happen" to find probabilities

Complement Rule

P(not A) = 1 - P(A)

• Sometimes easier to calculate the opposite

• "At least one" problems often use complements

• P(A) + P(not A) = 1 always

When to Use Complements

"At least one..."

Use: 1 - P(none)

"Not all..."

Use: 1 - P(all)

"Something happens"

Use: 1 - P(nothing happens)

Example: Coin Flips

P(at least 1 head in 3 flips)?

Direct way: Very complicated!

Complement: 1 - P(no heads)

P(no heads): P(all tails) = (1/2)³

P(all tails): 1/8 = 0.125

Answer: 1 - 0.125 = 0.875 or 87.5%

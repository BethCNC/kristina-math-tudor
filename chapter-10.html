Probability & Expected Value

Learn to quantify uncertainty and make informed decisions! Master probability calculations, expected values, and risk assessment for real-world situations.

Essential Probability Formulas

Basic Probability

P(event) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes

Always between 0 and 1

Certain event: P = 1 (100%)

Impossible event: P = 0 (0%)

Complement: P(not A) = 1 - P(A)

Expected Value

E(X) = Σ(x × P(x))

Sum of (outcome × its probability)

Interpretation: Long-term average

Use: Compare different options

Note: May not be a possible outcome

Tutorial: Basic Probability Concepts
What is Probability?

Probability measures how likely something is to happen. It's always a number between 0 and 1 (or 0% and 100%).

P = 0

Impossible

Rolling a 7 on a standard die

P = 0.5

50-50 chance

Flipping heads on a coin

P = 1

Certain

The sun rising tomorrow

Step-by-Step: Calculating Probability
Example: Rolling a die

Question: What's the probability of rolling a 3?

Step 1: Count favorable outcomes = 1 (only one way to roll a 3)

Step 2: Count total outcomes = 6 (faces 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6)

Step 3: Calculate: P(3) = 1/6 ≈ 0.167 or 16.7%

Complement Rule

Sometimes it's easier to calculate the probability of something NOT happening, then subtract from 1.

Example: At least one head in 3 coin flips

P(at least 1 head) = 1 - P(no heads)

P(no heads) = P(all tails) = (1/2)³ = 1/8

P(at least 1 head) = 1 - 1/8 = 7/8 = 0.875

Tutorial: Expected Value
What is Expected Value?

Expected value is the average outcome you'd expect if you repeated an experiment many times. It helps you make decisions by comparing long-term results.

Formula Breakdown:

E(X) = x₁P(x₁) + x₂P(x₂) + ... + xₙP(xₙ)

Multiply each outcome by its probability, then add them all up

Step-by-Step Example: Lottery Ticket

A $2 lottery ticket has these outcomes:

Win $1000

P = 1/10,000

Win $10

P = 50/10,000

Win $0

P = 9,949/10,000

Calculation:

E(X) = $1000 × (1/10,000) + $10 × (50/10,000) + $0 × (9,949/10,000)

E(X) = $0.10 + $0.05 + $0 = $0.15

Expected loss = $2.00 - $0.15 = $1.85 per ticket

Practice Problems

Problem 1: Card Probability

What's the probability of drawing a heart from a standard 52-card deck?

Show Solution

Favorable outcomes: 13 hearts

Total outcomes: 52 cards

Probability: P(heart) = 13/52 = 1/4 = 0.25 or 25%

Problem 2: Expected Value Game

A game costs $3 to play. You win $15 with probability 1/6, win $6 with probability 1/3, or lose your $3 otherwise. What's the expected value?

Show Solution

Net outcomes: Win $12 (15-3), Win $3 (6-3), Lose $3

Probabilities: 1/6, 1/3, 1/2 (since 1 - 1/6 - 1/3 = 1/2)

Calculate: E(X) = $12×(1/6) + $3×(1/3) + (-$3)×(1/2)

E(X) = $2 + $1 - $1.50 = $1.50

Result: Expected gain of $1.50 per game

Problem 3: Complement Rule

In a group of 25 students, what's the probability that at least one student was born on your birthday (ignore leap years)?

Show Solution

Use complement: P(at least 1) = 1 - P(none)

P(one student doesn't share): 364/365

P(all 25 don't share): (364/365)²⁵ ≈ 0.9315

P(at least 1 shares): 1 - 0.9315 = 0.0685 or 6.85%

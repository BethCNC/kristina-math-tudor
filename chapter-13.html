13.1 Voting Methods

Learn different ways to conduct elections and their outcomes

Four Main Voting Methods

1
Plurality Method

Candidate with the most votes wins (doesn't need majority)

2
Majority Runoff

If no majority, eliminate all but top two and re-vote

3
Borda Count

Points awarded based on ranking (1st = most points)

4
Plurality with Elimination

Eliminate lowest vote-getter, redistribute votes until majority

Example: Student Council Election

Three candidates: Alex (A), Blake (B), Casey (C)
Voter preferences: 40% prefer A>B>C, 35% prefer C>B>A, 25% prefer B>C>A

Plurality Method:
  • ‚Ä¢ Alex: 40%
  • ‚Ä¢ Casey: 35%
  • ‚Ä¢ Blake: 25%
  • Winner: Alex
Borda Count:
  • ‚Ä¢ Alex: 40√ó3 + 35√ó1 + 25√ó2 = 205
  • ‚Ä¢ Blake: 40√ó2 + 35√ó2 + 25√ó3 = 225
  • ‚Ä¢ Casey: 40√ó1 + 35√ó3 + 25√ó1 = 170
  • Winner: Blake
Key Insight: Different voting methods can produce different winners with the same votes!
Practice Problems

Problem 1:

In an election with candidates A, B, and C, the votes are: A: 45%, B: 35%, C: 20%. Which candidate wins using plurality method?

Plurality method: Winner is candidate with the most votes

Compare votes: A: 45%, B: 35%, C: 20%

Candidate A has the most votes

Answer: Candidate A wins with 45% of votes

Problem 2:

A state has 8 representatives to allocate among 3 districts with populations: District A: 45,000, District B: 30,000, District C: 25,000. Use Hamilton's method to find the allocation.

Total population: 45,000 + 30,000 + 25,000 = 100,000

Standard divisor: 100,000 √∑ 8 = 12,500

Standard quotas: A: 45,000√∑12,500 = 3.6, B: 30,000√∑12,500 = 2.4, C: 25,000√∑12,500 = 2.0

Lower quotas: A: 3, B: 2, C: 2 (total = 7)

Remaining seat goes to largest decimal: A gets the 8th seat

Answer: A: 4, B: 2, C: 2

13.2 Apportionment Methods

Learn how to fairly distribute seats in representative bodies

Key Concepts

Standard Quota

Population √∑ Standard Divisor

How many seats a state "deserves"

Hamilton Method

Give each state its lower quota

Distribute remaining seats to largest remainders

Jefferson Method

Lower the divisor until exactly right number of seats

Favors larger states

Webster Method

Round to nearest whole number

Currently used by US Congress
